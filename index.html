CV

Mike Cummings

I am a PhD student in the Department of Combinatorics and Optimization at the University of Waterloo, working with Oliver Pechenik. My expected graduation year is 2028.

In 2024, I received my master's degree from McMaster University, where my supervisor was Jenna Rajchgot. My master's thesis studied classes of Hessenberg varieties using patch ideals.

Research

My research is in combinatorial algebraic geometry, and I enjoy using computational tools. Recently, I have been thinking about: geometric vertex decomposition, Frobenius splitting, Hessenberg varieties, toric ideals of graphs, and matrix Schubert varieties.

My research is partially supported by an NSERC Canada Graduate Scholarship and a University of Waterloo President's Graduate Scholarship.

  • In January, I gave a poster at CAAC 2026 at Dalhousie. In December, I was at the 2025 CMS Winter Meeting in Toronto attending the combinatorial algebraic geometry session.
