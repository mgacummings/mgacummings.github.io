CV

Mike Cummings

In June 2024, I received my master's degree from McMaster University under the supervision of Jenna Rajchgot.

I will be starting a PhD in the Department of Combinatorics and Optimization at the University of Waterloo in Fall 2024.

Research

My research studies commutative algebraic/algebro-geometric objects using computational and combinatorial tools. Recently, I have been using geometric vertex decomposition and Frobenius splitting to study Hessenberg varieties, toric ideals of graphs, and matrix Schubert varieties.

I am partially supported by an Ontario Graduate Scholarship and a Milos Novotny Fellowship.

Teaching

During Summer 2024, I taught Applications of Linear Algebra – Math 2LA3 at McMaster University. In the upcoming Fall 2024 term, I will be the TA for Introduction to Combinatorics (Non-Specialist Level) – Math 229 at the University of Waterloo.

Extras

With Justin Gebel, Dicle Mutlu, and Adele Padgett, I co-organized McMaster's Casual Math Seminar in 2023–2024. Outside of Hamilton Hall, I am in the pit band of the McMaster Engineering Musical.
